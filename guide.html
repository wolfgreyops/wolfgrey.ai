5 Ready-to-Use AI Prompts

These prompts work with ChatGPT, Claude, or any major AI tool. Copy them, fill in your details, and get immediate results.

1

Email Subject Line Generator

Sales

Use when: You need compelling email subject lines that get opened. Works for cold outreach, newsletters, or follow-ups.

I need 10 email subject lines for [TYPE OF EMAIL: cold outreach / newsletter / follow-up]. My business: [WHAT YOU DO] Target audience: [WHO YOU'RE EMAILING] Goal of the email: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO] Requirements: - Under 50 characters each - Create curiosity without being clickbait - Sound human, not salesy - Mix different approaches (question, number, direct, personalized) For each subject line, add a one-sentence explanation of why it works.
2

Customer Persona Builder

Marketing

Use when: You need to understand your ideal customer better. Great for targeting ads, writing copy, or training your team.

Create a detailed customer persona for my business. My business: [WHAT YOU SELL/DO] Industry: [YOUR INDUSTRY] Price point: [YOUR PRICING] Current customers: [DESCRIBE WHO BUYS FROM YOU NOW] Build a persona that includes: - Demographics (age, income, location, job title) - Psychographics (values, fears, aspirations) - Pain points (what keeps them up at night) - Buying triggers (what makes them say yes) - Objections (why they might say no) - Where they spend time online - How they describe their problem in their own words Make it specific and actionable, not generic.
3

Social Media Content Batch

Content

Use when: You need a week's worth of social content in one sitting. Saves hours of daily content creation.

Create 7 social media posts for [PLATFORM: LinkedIn / Twitter / Instagram]. My business: [WHAT YOU DO] Target audience: [WHO YOU'RE REACHING] Brand voice: [DESCRIBE YOUR TONE: professional / casual / witty / etc.] Goal: [AWARENESS / ENGAGEMENT / LEADS / etc.] For each post, include: - The post text (platform-appropriate length) - A hook that stops the scroll - A call-to-action - 3-5 relevant hashtags - Best time to post (general guidance) Mix content types: 2 educational, 2 storytelling, 2 promotional, 1 engagement/question.
4

Meeting Prep Briefing

Operations

Use when: You have an important meeting and want to walk in prepared. Works for sales calls, client meetings, or partner discussions.

Prepare a briefing for my upcoming meeting. Meeting type: [SALES CALL / CLIENT CHECK-IN / PARTNER DISCUSSION / etc.] Company I'm meeting with: [COMPANY NAME] Person I'm meeting: [NAME AND TITLE] Meeting goal: [WHAT I WANT TO ACHIEVE] Context: [ANY RELEVANT BACKGROUND] Create a briefing that includes: - 3 key talking points I should cover - 5 questions I should ask them - Potential objections and how to handle them - Research on the company (recent news, challenges in their industry) - Specific value I can offer based on their situation - Recommended next steps to propose Keep it concise - I should be able to review this in 5 minutes.
5

Process Documentation

Operations

Use when: You need to document a process so anyone can follow it. Essential for training, delegation, or scaling.

Create a standard operating procedure (SOP) for this process. Process name: [NAME OF THE PROCESS] Purpose: [WHY THIS PROCESS EXISTS] Who performs it: [ROLE/PERSON] How often: [DAILY / WEEKLY / AS NEEDED / etc.] Current steps (rough): [DESCRIBE WHAT HAPPENS] Create an SOP that includes: - Overview (1-2 sentences on what this process accomplishes) - Prerequisites (what's needed before starting) - Step-by-step instructions (numbered, clear, specific) - Decision points (if X happens, do Y) - Common mistakes to avoid - Quality checklist (how to verify it was done correctly) - Time estimate for completion Format it so a new hire could follow it on day one.
