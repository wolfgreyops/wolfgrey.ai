AI Meeting Notes: Never Take Notes Again

The complete workflow for capturing, summarizing, and actioning meetings with AI. Stay present in conversations while AI handles the documentation.

The Workflow

4 Steps to Meeting Freedom

From "I'll take notes" to "AI has it covered."

1

Record the Meeting

Use your video platform's built-in recording (Zoom, Meet, Teams) or a dedicated tool like Granola for in-person meetings. Always get consent from participants.

2

Get the Transcript

Most recording tools auto-generate transcripts. If not, upload the audio to a transcription service. You'll have a text file of everything said.

3

Feed It to Claude

Paste the transcript into Claude with one of the prompts below. Get a structured summary in seconds, not the 30+ minutes it takes to write one manually.

4

Distribute & Action

Send the summary to participants. Create tasks from action items. Store notes in your project folder. The meeting is officially complete.

Prompts

Meeting Summary Templates

Copy, paste, customize.

Standard Meeting Summary All-Purpose

Works for any meeting type. Gets you the essentials.

Summarize this meeting transcript. Include: 1. **Meeting Overview** (1-2 sentences: who, what, why) 2. **Key Discussion Points** (3-5 bullets of main topics covered) 3. **Decisions Made** (specific decisions with who made them) 4. **Action Items** (task, owner, deadline if mentioned) 5. **Open Questions** (unresolved items that need follow-up) Keep it concise. Use bullet points. Don't include small talk or off-topic discussion. [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]
Client Meeting Notes Sales/Client

Optimized for client calls where you need to track requirements and commitments.

Summarize this client meeting transcript. Focus on: 1. **Client Name & Context** (who they are, what they need) 2. **Their Pain Points** (problems they mentioned, in their words) 3. **Requirements Discussed** (what they want us to do) 4. **Our Commitments** (what we said we'd deliver) 5. **Their Concerns** (objections, hesitations, questions) 6. **Next Steps** (who does what by when) 7. **Notable Quotes** (2-3 direct quotes that capture their priorities) Flag anything that sounds like scope creep or a red flag. [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]
Team Standup Summary Internal

For daily/weekly standups where you need to track blockers and progress.

Summarize this team standup. Format as: **[Team Member Name]** - Completed: [what they finished] - Working on: [current focus] - Blockers: [anything stopping progress] Then add: **Team Blockers** (issues affecting multiple people) **Dependencies** (who's waiting on whom) **Priorities for This Week** (based on discussion) Keep each person's section to 3-4 bullets max. [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]
Action Items Extractor Task Focus

When you just need the to-dos, nothing else.

Extract all action items from this meeting transcript. For each item include: - [ ] **Task**: [specific action to take] - **Owner**: [who's responsible] - **Deadline**: [date if mentioned, or "TBD"] - **Context**: [one sentence on why this matters] Only include explicit commitments ("I'll do X" or "Can you handle Y"). Don't infer tasks that weren't clearly assigned. [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]

Pro Tip: Meeting Prep

Before your next meeting, ask Claude: "Based on these notes from our last meeting with [CLIENT], what questions should I prepare? What commitments do I need to follow up on?" Context from past meetings makes current meetings more productive.
